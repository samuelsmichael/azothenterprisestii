<ponderence>Identifying is the chief obstacle to self-remembering. In order to learn not to identify man must first of all not be identified with himself, must not call himself &ldquo;I&rdquo; always and on all occasions. He must remember that there are two in him, that there is himself, that is &ldquo;I&rdquo; in him, and another with whom he must struggle and whom he must conquer.</ponderence>

<topractice>Gurdjieff gave an exercise in one of the Paris meetings, which consisted of five minutes of work in the morning and five minutes in the evening. 
Try to do this when there are not a lot of external influences pulling on you. The exercise consists of expanding your self-awareness out of the localization of the head and dropping it down into the chest and lower body. 
Try to connect with the whole of yourself, i.e. this is myself, in this moment, &ldquo;I.&rdquo; Try not to think this exercise, but to sense and feel your own presence.</topractice>
